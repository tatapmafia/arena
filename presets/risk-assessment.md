# Risk Assessment Preset

Best for: investment decisions, project go/no-go, strategy stress-testing, due diligence, any high-stakes bet.

## Why these 3 roles

Most risk analysis is either too optimistic (confirmation bias) or too pessimistic (fear-driven). The three-perspective model forces the real conversation: the optimist makes the best case, the pessimist attacks it, and the realist anchors both to reality. The consensus reveals whether the plan survives adversarial scrutiny.

## Example questions

- "Should we raise a Series A now or wait 6 months?"
- "Is this acquisition worth 3x revenue?"
- "Should we bet the company on AI features?"
- "Do we kill the underperforming product line?"
- "Should we open an office in Europe?"

## Calibration notes

- For technical risk, add a 4th agent: "engineer" (someone who evaluates implementation feasibility and hidden complexity).
- For decisions involving people/culture, add: "culture-keeper" (someone who evaluates impact on team morale, hiring, and retention).
- The pessimist role should be assigned as devil's advocate in round 1 to set the adversarial tone early.
