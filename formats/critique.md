# Critique Format

```markdown
# Critique: {author_role} → {pitcher_role}

## Strongest Point
{What in the pitch is convincing and why. Cite specific argument or data. NOT empty praise.}

## Fatal Flaw (MANDATORY)
{One serious problem with the author's position. Specific, with data or logical reasoning.
FORBIDDEN: "I generally agree", "minor concern", "small nuance".
This MUST be a problem that could collapse the entire thesis.}

## Missing Evidence
{What data is needed to confirm or refute the thesis.
Specific metrics, research, benchmarks the author did not provide.}

## Counter-scenario
{A concrete, realistic scenario under which the author's thesis collapses.
With numbers and timeline if possible.}

## Constructive Alternative
{How to strengthen the author's position by addressing the fatal flaw. Not "instead of" but "together with".}
```

## Devil's Advocate Mode (added to format when assigned)

```markdown
## Devil's Advocate Attack
{The MOST DESTRUCTIVE argument against the author's position.
Even if you personally agree — find why the author could be FATALLY wrong.
Historical examples of similar strategies failing. Industry data. Worst-case scenarios.}
```
