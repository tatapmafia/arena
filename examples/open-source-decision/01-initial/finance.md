# Initial Analysis: Finance

## Position: Against (Protect Revenue Potential)

### Competitive Advantage at Risk

KubeDeploy's canary scoring model is our differentiator. Releasing it as OSS means:
- Any competitor can fork it and build a hosted version
- Our planned KubeDeploy Cloud loses its technical moat
- We give away 18 months of R&D investment (~$400K in engineering time)

### Revenue Math

KubeDeploy Cloud projected revenue (conservative):
- Year 1: $500K ARR (20 enterprise customers at $25K)
- Year 2: $2M ARR
- If competitor forks and undercuts by 30%, we lose ~40% of that pipeline

### Hidden Costs of OSS

- Community management: $150K/yr (1 DevRel + tooling)
- Security response SLA: dedicated on-call for CVEs
- Legal review of contributions (IP contamination risk)
- Conference presence to maintain credibility: $50K/yr

**Total incremental cost: ~$250-300K/yr**

### What Could Change My Mind

- If the hosted offering's value is primarily in the platform layer (multi-tenant, billing, SSO), not in the deployment logic itself
- If OSS adoption directly feeds the hosted pipeline (prove it with data from comparable companies)

### Confidence: 70% against
