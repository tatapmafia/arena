# Initial Analysis: Strategist

## Position: Strongly Pro Open-Source

### Hiring Leverage

Our biggest bottleneck is hiring. Open-source projects are the #1 driver of inbound engineering talent for infrastructure companies. Argo (Intuit), Envoy (Lyft), and Vitess (YouTube) all followed this pattern. We're competing for the same engineers.

**Estimate:** A well-received OSS project reduces cost-per-hire by 30-40% and increases inbound applications by 2-3x for infrastructure roles.

### Brand & Market Position

KubeCon talks about our approach generated real interest. Open-sourcing converts that interest into:
- Community validation of our architecture
- Integration partnerships (CI/CD vendors building plugins)
- A moat around the hosted offering (community = lock-in)

### Strategic Timing

If competitors build a similar tool first and open-source it, we lose the first-mover advantage permanently. The window is 6-12 months.

### Risks I Acknowledge

- Maintenance load will increase
- We need to strip internal config service dependencies
- Competitor forking is possible but manageable

### Confidence: 75% in favor
